The Modern Yogi Toolset

Best Yoga Accessories for Home or Studio

The essential yoga accessories include a high-grip natural rubber mat, two firm blocks (cork or dense foam), and a non-stretch cotton strap. These tools improve alignment, extend your reach, and provide safety.

Lisa Marie
20+ years of devoted practice

The Foundation: Why We Use Props

There is a common beginner’s ego that whispers, "Props are for people who can't do the pose." Lisa Marie and the modern yoga community challenge this. Props are not "crutches"; they are extensions of the body that allow for better proprioception (the body's ability to sense movement and location).

A block brings the floor to you, allowing your spine to stay long. A strap acts as an extension of your arm, preventing you from straining your neck to reach your toes. Props allow the pose to fit the body, rather than forcing the body to fit a magazine-perfect shape.

Essential vs. Supportive Gear

The Essentials

  • High-Grip Mat: Natural rubber or TPE.
  • Cork Blocks: For weight-bearing support.
  • Cotton Strap: 8-10ft with a D-ring.

The Supportive (Home)

  • Rectangular Bolster: For restorative poses.
  • Woven Blanket: For knee padding.
  • Eye Pillow: For deep Savasana.

Choosing Materials: Cork vs. Foam

Cork blocks are the choice for the modern, environmentally conscious yogi. They are firm, sustainable, and provide a secure grip even when damp. They are ideal for standing poses where you need to lean your weight into the block.

Foam blocks are lightweight and softer. These are better for restorative practices where you might be resting your head or spine on the block for long durations. They are less durable but more affordable for beginners.
