# **Strategic Blueprint: The Modern Yogi Manual**

**Vision:** Shift the site's identity from a "Search Engine" to a "Curated Concierge."

---

## **I. Naming & Brand Positioning**

To move away from the cold, technical feel of a directory, rename your FAQ assets to reflect an editorial, high-end guide.

* **Internal Label:** "The Modern Yogi Manual" or "The Flow Guide."  
* **Navigation Label:** "Guides" or "The Manual" (Avoid using "FAQ" in primary navigation).  
* **Voice & Tone:** Knowledgeable, practical, urban-chic, and encouraging.

---

## **III. URL Structure & Site Hierarchy**

To ensure SEO health and LLM (AI) discoverability, use a flat, descriptive URL structure.

* **The Answer Silo URL:** yoganearme.info/answers/\[question-slug\]/  
  * *Example:* yoganearme.info/answers/what-to-wear-to-first-yoga-class/  
*   
* **Alternative (Guide Style):** yoganearme.info/guides/\[question-slug\]/  
* **Avoid:** Numeric IDs (e.g., /hatha-yoga-12) or deep nesting (e.g., /faq/beginners/questions/slug).

---

## **IV. Page Format & Optimization Strategy**

Each of the 30 pages must be a "Mini-Guide" to rank as a high-authority asset.

### **1\. Content Requirements**

* **Word Count:** 800–1,200 words.  
* **The "Snippet Bait":** A 40–60 word direct answer placed immediately under the H1 header.  
* **Rich Media:** 2–3 high-quality lifestyle images with descriptive Alt Text and one embedded YouTube video.

### **2\. The Page Layout (SEO/LLM Framework)**

* **H1 Header:** The Question (e.g., *What to Wear to Your First Yoga Class*)  
* **Summary Paragraph:** \[Snippet Bait\]  
* **Table of Contents:** Jump-links to sub-headers.  
* **H2 Sections:** Break the topic down (e.g., *Fabric Choices*, *What to Avoid*, *Footwear*).  
* **Contextual CTA:** A link back to your directory (e.g., *"Find an urban sanctuary in NYC"*).  
* **Schema Markup:** Use **FAQ Schema (JSON-LD)** to tell Google and AI exactly what the question and answer are.

---

## **V. Navigation & Internal Linking**

The "Concierge" does not hide information; they present it exactly when the user needs it.

* **Primary Navigation:** Keep "Find a Studio," "Styles," and "Guides" at the top.  
* **Footer:** Place the full "Manual" or "FAQ" index here.  
* **Contextual Linking (The Secret Sauce):**  
  * On a **Studio Listing Page**, add an accordion or sidebar: *"New to this? Read our guide on \[What to Bring to Class\]."*  
  * On a **City Page**, add a section: *"Yoga in \[City\]: Pricing & Logistics. \[Read our Guide to Studio Costs\]."*
