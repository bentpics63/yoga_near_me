## Workflow 1: Glossary Term Pages

### Overview
- **Target length:** ~600 words
- **Voice:** Lisa Marie
- **Purpose:** Define yoga terminology accessibly while providing practice context

### Pre-Production Checklist

- [ ] Confirm term spelling and common variations
- [ ] Research Sanskrit pronunciation (phonetic guide)
- [ ] Identify 6-8 related terms for cross-linking
- [ ] Check existing glossary for consistency
- [ ] Review any completed term pages for structure reference

### Content Structure

```markdown
# [Term Name]

**Pronunciation:** [phonetic guide]

[1-2 sentence compelling subtitle that draws reader in]

---

## What Is [Term]?

[Clear, accessible definition — 2-3 paragraphs]
[Ground in tradition without academic distance]

## [Term] in Practice

[What this means in a class setting]
[How students encounter this concept]
[Practical application]

## Why [Term] Matters

[Benefits and importance — can use bullets]
- Physical benefit
- Mental/emotional benefit  
- Spiritual/philosophical benefit

## Variations & Contexts

[Different applications or interpretations]
[How this appears across different yoga styles]

## Tips for Practice

> [Callout box with practical guidance]
> [Keep it actionable and encouraging]

---

## Related Terms

- [Term 1] — [brief description]
- [Term 2] — [brief description]
- [Term 3] — [brief description]
- [Term 4] — [brief description]
- [Term 5] — [brief description]
- [Term 6] — [brief description]
```

### SEO Requirements

- **Title tag:** `[Term] - Definition, Meaning & Practice | Yoga Near Me`
- **Meta description:** 150-160 chars, include term + brief definition + invitation
- **H1:** Term name only
- **Schema:** DefinedTerm + Article + BreadcrumbList
- **Internal links:** Related terms, relevant style pages, benefits articles

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Passes the Lisa Test (would she say this to a student?)
- [ ] Sanskrit used correctly and defined on first use
- [ ] No wellness buzzwords or pseudoscience
- [ ] Tradition/history mentioned where relevant
- [ ] Practical application is clear
- [ ] All related terms are actual glossary entries
- [ ] Meta tags complete

## Workflow 2: Yoga Style Pages

### Overview
- **Target length:** 2,000-3,000 words
- **Voice:** Lisa Marie
- **Purpose:** Comprehensive guide to a yoga style that helps practitioners decide if it's right for them

### Pre-Production Checklist

- [ ] Research style origins and key figures
- [ ] Identify signature poses/sequences
- [ ] Understand breath-movement relationship
- [ ] Note what makes this style distinct from similar styles
- [ ] Identify 3-4 related styles for cross-linking
- [ ] Select appropriate hero image

### Content Structure

```markdown
# [Style Name] Yoga

[Poetic tagline — one line that captures the essence]

---

## Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Pace | [Slow/Moderate/Fast] |
| Intensity | [Gentle/Moderate/Vigorous] |
| Heat | [Room temp/Warm/Hot] |
| Focus | [Alignment/Flow/Breath/etc.] |
| Best for | [Beginners/Intermediate/Advanced/All levels] |

---

## What Is [Style] Yoga?

[2-3 paragraphs defining the style]
[How it feels in the body]
[What distinguishes it from other styles]

## Origins & Philosophy

[Historical context — where, when, who]
[Key figures and lineage]
[Philosophical foundation]
[How tradition informs modern practice]

## Style & Structure

[How a typical class unfolds]
[Signature sequences or poses]
[What to expect in your first class]

## Breath & Flow

[Relationship between breath and movement]
[Specific pranayama techniques used]
[How breath guides the practice]

## Pace, Focus & Teacher Role

[Energy level and rhythm]
[Where attention is directed]
[How teachers typically guide]
[Adjustments and modifications]

## The Vibe

[Atmosphere and feeling]
[Music, lighting, community]
[What the room feels like]

---

## Benefits of [Style] Yoga

[Brief intro — 1-2 sentences]

### Physical Benefits
- [Benefit 1] — [1 sentence description]
- [Benefit 2] — [1 sentence description]
- [Benefit 3] — [1 sentence description]

### Mental & Emotional Benefits
- [Benefit 1] — [1 sentence description]
- [Benefit 2] — [1 sentence description]
- [Benefit 3] — [1 sentence description]

[Link to full "12 Benefits of [Style] Yoga" article]

---

## Who Is [Style] Yoga For?

[Who thrives in this practice]
[Who might want to start elsewhere]
[Honest assessment — not everyone needs every style]

## Finding [Style] Classes Near You

[Brief CTA to studio search]
[What to look for in a [Style] studio]

---

## Closing Reflection

[Philosophical conclusion — 2-3 paragraphs]
[Deeper meaning of the practice]
[Invitation to begin or continue]

## Related Styles

- **[Style 1]** — [Why it's related, 1 sentence]

- **[Style 3]** — [Why it's related, 1 sentence]
```

### SEO Requirements

- **Title tag:** `[Style] Yoga - Complete Guide: Benefits, Origins & What to Expect`
- **Meta description:** 150-160 chars, style name + key benefit + invitation
- **H1:** [Style] Yoga
- **Schema:** Article + BreadcrumbList + FAQPage (if FAQ included)
- **Internal links:** Related styles, glossary terms used, local search

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Passes the Lisa Test
- [ ] Tradition and history are properly honored
- [ ] Physical benefits don't overshadow other benefits
- [ ] No fitness industry framing
- [ ] Sanskrit terms defined on first use
- [ ] Related styles are accurate comparisons
- [ ] Hero image is appropriate and properly attributed
- [ ] All internal links work

## Workflow 3: Business Articles (Studio Owner Content)

### Overview
- **Target length:** 1,500-2,500 words
- **Voice:** Eddie
- **Purpose:** Help studio owners solve specific business problems with data-backed solutions

### Pre-Production Checklist

- [ ] Identify the specific pain point
- [ ] Gather supporting data/statistics
- [ ] Find or create relevant case study
- [ ] Determine clear action steps
- [ ] Identify internal linking opportunities

### Content Structure

```markdown
# [Problem-Focused Title with Specific Hook]

[1-2 sentence hook naming the pain point and quantifying stakes]

---

## The Problem

[What's broken — be specific]
[Why it matters — quantify the cost]
[Data showing scope of the problem]

## What's Actually Happening

[Deeper analysis — the "McPhee" section]
[Research and context]
[Why conventional solutions don't work]

## The Solution

[Clear, actionable answer]
[No hedging — be direct (Vonnegut)]
[Why this approach works]

## How to Implement

### Step 1: [Action]
[Detailed instructions]

### Step 2: [Action]
[Detailed instructions]

### Step 3: [Action]
[Detailed instructions]

[Continue as needed]

## The Results

[Case study or data showing outcomes]
[Specific numbers and timeframes]
[What studios can realistically expect]

## What This Means for Your Studio

[Practical takeaways]
[Next steps]
[How to get started today]

---

## Related Resources

- [Internal link 1]
- [Internal link 2]
- [Tool or service if relevant]
```

### SEO Requirements

- **Title tag:** 50-60 chars, keyword-forward, benefit-oriented
- **Meta description:** 150-160 chars, problem + solution + CTA
- **H1:** Match title or close variant
- **Schema:** Article + BreadcrumbList
- **Internal links:** Related business articles, relevant tools, glossary if applicable

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Passes the Eddie Test (data, action, ROI, peer-level tone)
- [ ] Specific numbers throughout (not vague claims)
- [ ] Clear action steps a reader can implement today
- [ ] No consultant-speak or corporate jargon
- [ ] Case study or proof included
- [ ] CTA is helpful, not pushy

## Workflow 4: Benefits Articles ("12 Benefits of [X]")

### Overview
- **Target length:** 1,200-1,800 words
- **Voice:** Lisa Marie
- **Purpose:** Outline specific benefits of a yoga style or practice in an accessible, honest way

### Pre-Production Checklist

- [ ] Identify 12 benefits across categories (physical, mental, emotional, energetic)
- [ ] Ensure benefits are grounded in experience, not pseudoscience
- [ ] Prepare honest scope language (no miracle claims)
- [ ] Draft FAQ questions beginners actually ask

### Content Structure

```markdown
# 12 Benefits of [Style/Practice]

[Brief philosophical context — 1-2 sentences about the practice]

---

## 1. [Benefit Name]

[Poetic statement + 1-2 sentence description]
[Grounded in embodied experience]

## 2. [Benefit Name]

[Continue pattern...]

[Repeat through 12 benefits]

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [Common beginner question]?

[Practical, honest answer]

### [Common beginner question]?

[Practical, honest answer]

[3-5 FAQs total]

---

## Ready to Experience These Benefits?

[Invitation to find classes — soft CTA]
[Link to style page for more information]
```

### Benefit Categories (Mix Across All 12)

**Physical:** Flexibility, strength, balance, posture, breathing capacity
**Mental:** Focus, stress reduction, clarity, presence
**Emotional:** Emotional regulation, self-awareness, resilience
**Energetic/Spiritual:** Connection, grounding, vitality (grounded, not woo)

### Language Guidelines

- Use "gradually," "naturally," "sustainable" — not miracle language
- Speak to embodied experience, not abstract claims
- Honest scope — yoga helps, it doesn't cure everything
- Each benefit should feel like Lisa Marie explaining to a student

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Passes the Lisa Test
- [ ] No overselling or miracle claims
- [ ] Benefits span multiple categories
- [ ] FAQs answer real beginner questions
- [ ] Honest about what yoga can and can't do

## SEO Checklist (All Content Types)

### Technical Requirements

- [ ] Title tag: 50-60 characters
- [ ] Meta description: 150-160 characters
- [ ] H1: Matches or closely matches title
- [ ] H2s: Include relevant keywords naturally
- [ ] Images: Descriptive filenames, alt text, compressed
- [ ] Internal links: 3-5 minimum to relevant content
- [ ] External links: Only to authoritative sources when needed
- [ ] Schema markup: Appropriate type for content

### E-E-A-T Signals

- [ ] Author byline with credentials
- [ ] "Written by" or "Reviewed by" attribution
- [ ] Links to author bio page
- [ ] Publication date visible
- [ ] Update date if revised

### On-Page Optimization

- [ ] Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words
- [ ] Related keywords distributed naturally
- [ ] Readable paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- [ ] Subheadings every 200-300 words
- [ ] Bulleted lists where appropriate (not overused)
