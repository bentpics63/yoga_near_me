Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about finding yoga studios, booking classes, and starting your practice.

To find a yoga studio near you, enter your city, address, or postal code into our search bar. You can also browse by region or city from our home page. Our directory uses your location (with permission) to show studios closest to you, making it easy to discover options in both the U.S. and Canada. Use our filters to refine results by style for a personalized experience.

Our directory features all major yoga styles including Hatha, Vinyasa, Ashtanga, Yin, Hot Yoga (26+2), Power Yoga, Restorative, Iyengar, Kundalini, Prenatal, Aerial, and Rocket Yoga. Each studio listing shows which styles they offer, and you can filter your search by specific styles to find exactly what you're looking for.

Each studio listing includes direct contact information, website links, and phone numbers. Click on any studio to view their full profile, then use the provided contact methods to book classes directly with them. We connect you with studios but don't handle bookings ourselves - this ensures you get the most current pricing and availability directly from the source.

Yes! Many studios in our directory offer beginner-friendly classes. Look for styles like Hatha, Restorative, Yin, and Gentle Yoga which are particularly welcoming to newcomers. Studio profiles often indicate if they offer beginner classes, and you can contact studios directly to ask about introductory packages or trial classes designed for first-timers.

For your first class, bring: a yoga mat (though many studios provide these for rent or loan), comfortable clothing that allows movement, a water bottle, and a small towel if practicing hot yoga. Arrive 10-15 minutes early to complete any paperwork and introduce yourself to the instructor. Most studios provide props like blocks and straps, but check their website or call ahead to confirm what's included.

Pricing varies by location and studio type. Drop-in classes typically range from $15-30 per class. Monthly unlimited memberships range from $80-200. Urban studios in major cities tend to be pricier than suburban or smaller-town studios. Many studios offer intro packages (like 30 days for $30) that provide excellent value for new students. Check individual studio listings for current pricing and special offers.

We encourage you to leave reviews on the studio's Google Business Profile, Yelp, or Facebook page - these help other students make informed decisions and support great studios. You can find links to these platforms on each studio's listing page. Your honest feedback helps build a stronger yoga community!

We regularly update our directory with information from studios, user reports, and public sources. Studios can claim their listings to update their information directly. If you notice outdated information, please contact us so we can verify and update the listing. We're committed to providing the most accurate, current information possible to help you find the perfect yoga practice.
